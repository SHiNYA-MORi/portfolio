Shinya Mori

I design coordination mechanisms for the commons.

He explores plural futures where people with diverse values respect each other and embrace non-anthropocentric perspectives. His work is grounded in open/free culture and eastern philosophy—animism, taoism, and buddhism—that honors interconnectedness beyond human-centric worldviews.

Focus Areas

He works in public goods and commons, focusing on coordination mechanisms that enable collective action:

  • Public goods funding mechanisms
  • Impact evaluation frameworks
  • Collective decision making processes
  • Governance of commons
  • Credible neutrality in systems
  • Decentralized democratic governance
